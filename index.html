Privacy Policy

Last updated: 14 July 2026

Delulu Kart is a shop where nothing is ever sold. You fill a cart, you check out, and you are charged nothing - because nothing was ever for sale. The app celebrates the money you didn't spend.

An app like that would be a bit ridiculous if it quietly harvested your data in the background. So it doesn't. This page explains exactly what happens to your information, in plain language.

The short version

  • No sign-up, no login, no account. There is nowhere to enter an email or a phone number, because we never ask for one.
  • No real payments, ever. Checkout is entirely fictional. There is no payment gateway, no bank, no card, no UPI. Nothing to charge and nothing to store.
  • Your cart, wishlist, orders and achievements live on your phone. Not on our servers. Uninstall the app and they are gone for good.
  • What you post publicly is public. Reviews and Dream Carts are shown to other people in the app, under the nickname you picked.

We never ask for, and never collect

  • Your real name
  • Your email address
  • Your phone number
  • Any password or account credential
  • Any payment information of any kind
  • Your location
  • Your contacts, photos, or files

There is no screen in the app that asks for any of these. The delivery address you see at checkout is invented by the app itself as part of the joke it is not real, and it never leaves your phone.

Your nickname

When you first open the app you pick a nickname - something like SilentSaver64. You make it up, or the app suggests one. It is not linked to your real identity, and we have no way to connect it to you.

If you post a review or enter a Dream Cart, that nickname is shown next to it, so other people can see who wrote what. If you never post anything, your nickname stays on your phone and we never see it at all.

The random ID your phone makes

To keep the app fair, we need to know that two votes came from two different phones - not one person voting twice. So the first time it's needed, your phone generates a random ID for itself. Something like a3f1c9e2-7b4d-4c8a-9e1f-2d5b8c7a6e3f.

Two things are worth knowing about it:

  • It is not your phone's identity. It is not the IMEI, not the Android ID, not an advertising ID. It is a random number your phone invented. It says nothing about you, your device, or where you are.
  • It disappears when you uninstall. There is no way to recover it, and no way for us to link a new one to an old one. Reinstall the app and, as far as the app is concerned, you are a brand new person.

We only ever see this ID if you do something - post a review, enter a Dream Cart, cast a vote. If you just browse the shop, we never see it, and there is no record anywhere that you were here.

Things you post publicly

Some parts of the app are shared with everyone. If you take part in these, what you post is stored on our servers and shown to other people:

  • Reviews - your star rating, what you wrote, and your nickname.
  • Dream Carts and Challenges - the imaginary cart you built, the name you gave it, and your nickname.
  • Reactions and votes - these are counted, but shown as totals only. Nobody can see how you personally voted.
  • Hall of Fame - if your review or cart is popular enough, it is kept there permanently.

Please don't put personal details : your real name, phone number, email, address - into a review or a cart name. Anyone using the app will be able to see them.

To keep these spaces pleasant, submitted text is checked automatically for abusive content, and anyone can report a post. Posts reported by enough people are hidden.

Notifications

The app can send you notifications - a nudge after you first join, or a note when your cart makes it into the Hall of Fame.

These work a little unusually, and we're rather proud of it: we cannot send a notification to your phone. We have no way to reach it. Instead, your phone asks us "is there anything for me?" and shows the message itself. There is no push token, no address, nothing stored that could be used to contact you. The message only arrives because your phone went and fetched it.

You can turn notifications off in your phone's settings at any time.

The fictional checkout

The checkout is a performance. No order is placed. No payment is processed. No card number, UPI ID, or billing address is collected, transmitted, or stored - the app never asks for them and has nowhere to send them. The delivery details shown are generated by the app for comic effect and stay on your phone.

The only thing recorded is an anonymous tally: how many different phones "bought" a given item. That's what powers the "247 people have imagined owning this" line. It's a count, and nothing more.

Technical data

Like any app, Delulu Kart runs on ordinary platform and hosting services, which keep standard operational logs - things like app version, crash reports, and error logs. We use these to fix bugs. We do not use them to identify you, and we do not sell or share them for advertising.

Images

Product images are served from a content delivery network. Loading them makes an ordinary internet request, as any web page does. We receive no personal information from that process.

Advertising

Some products are marked with an "Ad" label. That's a promoted item in our own catalogue - nothing more. There is no advertising network in the app, no tracking, no profiling, and nothing about you is shared with anyone for advertising purposes.

Children

Delulu Kart is not directed at children and does not knowingly collect personal information from them. Since we don't collect personal information from anyone, this is a promise the app keeps by design.

Your choices

  • Delete everything - uninstall the app, or clear its data in your phone's settings. Your cart, wishlist, orders, achievements and random ID are erased immediately and permanently.
  • Remove something you posted - public posts are tied only to a random ID, so we can't look them up for you. Email us with the details and we'll do our best to find and remove it.
  • Turn off notifications - in your phone's settings, any time.

Changes to this policy

If the app changes in a way that affects any of this, we'll update this page and change the date at the top. If we ever introduced accounts, real payments, or analytics - none of which are planned - we would say so here, plainly.
